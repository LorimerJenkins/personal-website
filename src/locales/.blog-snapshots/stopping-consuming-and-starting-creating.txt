# Intro

I made a promise to myself at the start of the year to start creating content, it is now March 15th and I have not produced too much.

This blog is going to serve as a rough debrief for me on things that have worked, not worked and general things that didn’t/did work so I can see what I need to improve on.

For the past few months I have been experimenting with various content formats/styles.

- Work content
- General life updates
- Trends

I feel now I have some good data on what works and what doesn’t from view counts and likes. The things that have seemed to work for me is when I tell stories, where I am simply a character in a story. 

For example I did 3 or 4 reels speaking to the camera giving out general information, they flopped. 

But I then did one reel on acting out one of my experiences (a story) on fundraising and that seemed to go down quite well. But to be fair, me putting on a bad American accent may have contributed to that. 

Content I make that has a slight entertainment value seems to work well. 

# Looking at my piers

I have started attending more founder networking dinners across London, since then I have met a few fellow founders creating content. From talking to them, one had gotten 10x more followers than me in a week doing daily posts, than I did on and off in 3 months.

I know it is not good to compare yourself to others, but I feel looking at other people trying a similar thing to me for tips and not to criticise myself is ok.

I also know of another friend who has a more professional setup, they have a strong daily consistancy and have amassed over 50k followers over the past few months - when I first started they had around 3k.

It is definitely possible to grow followers, I need to just figure out how.

Looking at them I am trying to figure out what they are doing that seems to be working that I need to do. 

Here are some observations:

- Daily consistency seems to be a huge factor, the people I know doing well on social media are creating content everyday in their routine.

- Story telling, the creators I know raking in followers tell stories. They act, they are not always natural humans - they usually act out characters and tell stories. 

- The thumbnail counts, if my reels don’t have an interesting front cover, people will not watch them . Reels are not books, people judge them by its cover. 

- Time investment, people will not put in time investment when scrolling through reels for bad videos. I’m now learning content creation for the first time so making long reels is a bad move until I know what works and doesn’t. I started off with longer 1-2 min reels, it now seems shorter 30s reels do far better. 

- Creation time, reels take me a couple hours to make from filming, editing and posting. I don’t know if that is because I am really slow, which may be the case, but it seems I need to allocate more time out of my day to making them. I know creators who have whole teams and agencies behind them. I am not ruling out experimenting with that, but now it seems I need to spend more time everyday creating them - unless I am willing to pay for delegation to do it. 

- Stop watching reels, I do actually watch TikTok/Instagram reels daily. I need to stop it if I want to be a creator. There is a real switch I need to put in my brain to stop consuming content and instead start creating it. 

# The new direction

I am now refocussing and feel I need to fundamentally make a change in how I am approaching content. I need to allocate time in the day to do this and do it everyday. I need to create stories, play characters, get quicker at producing them and stop spending so much time everyday watching other peoples content. 

The thing I find hardest is consistency, as with learning anything, I need to practise practise practise. 

When I taught myself to code I made a daily habit of spending at least 4 hours a day on creating content, I need to do something like that with content. I need to spend at least 2 hours every morning creating content before I do anything else to make sure it gets done. 

I am now going to be renting an office for 3 months as a trail rather than working from home. I think this will be a good life change. I will now wake up, eat, train into London, get to the office and create content first thing. Then do my normal work stuff for the rest of the day and I don’t have to then finish work quickly to make a reel or make a bad reel from rushing it last thing. 

Thinking out loud maybe I should write the content on the train down, record it fresh as soon as I get into work and then edit and post it immediately. 

Whenever learning new things I find focusing on really simple core objectives to achieve. So from now on my simple objectives will be: 


- Create content first thing everyday to make sure it is done and define a really quick production process. Then it is done and more of a daily routine like brushing my teeth
- I need to tell stories, be a character in a story and make content fun to watch as well as interesting. 

I may be going down the wrong path here, but I feel making a change is what I need right now. I will now spend a few months in the new directions and see how it goes. But I feel good that I am still actively trying to do content and not just stop after a week. 

I just have to keep going and not stop.
